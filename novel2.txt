Every evening, Meera crossed the old railway bridge on her way home.

No trains had passed there in years.

Yet one day, as the fog settled, she heard it — the distant rumble of wheels.

A train appeared, slow and silent, its windows glowing faintly.

Without thinking, she stepped inside.

There were no passengers. Only empty seats and a ticking clock.

At the next stop, she got down.

But nothing looked familiar anymore.

The bridge was gone.

And so was her way back.
